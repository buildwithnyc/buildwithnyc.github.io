Product Management Fellow

The Product Management Fellow will focus on supporting My File NYC, an open source site that allows residents to store and share documents for social services. It gives residents control of their personal information, reduces the time for establishing eligibility for benefits, and reduces the repetition of the document upload process each time residents apply for services.

They will also support other products including new tools to help visualize the City’s poverty data to encourage data-driven decision making and transparency.

  Responsibilities

The Product Management Fellow will work alongside the team to:

  • Support pilot operations including training, in-person assistance and stakeholder communication.
  • Work with the Product Manager, Product Director, UX Designers, Content Strategist and Engineers to plan and facilitate sprints.
  • Plan and perform site / application quality assurance.
  • Review and organize user feedback, turning it into actionable items for the team to work on.
  • Prepare analytics reports using quantitative and qualitative data.
  • Support the planning and participating in multichannel user feedback methods / sessions.
  • Produce and maintain product documentation: backlog, product specs, tickets, wikis, etc

  Skills We're Seeking

We understand that candidates may not have all of the skills we are seeking below. We encourage those interested to apply even if they do not meet all of them. We are looking for talented individuals to join our team.

Commitment to the mission of advancing greater equality and opportunity through best-in-class digital products and human-centered design practices in government.

Comfortable working with vulnerable populations, including in-person or virtual engagements.

Previous experience with product management, project management, or content design and management involving the design and development of technology projects.

Ability to develop metrics and produce reports for products and initiatives.

Ability to turn user research insights and data into new product features or enhancements.

Technical and design fluency to support product vision and development.

  Education and Experience

Fellowships are open to current college or graduate students and recent graduates (or equivalent) with preferred work experience in these disciplines: product management and development, content design and management, civic tech, public policy, and social impact.

Applicants must be authorized to work in the United States. We are unable to provide sponsorship to fellows.

  Compensation and Schedule

The fellowship pays $27.50/per hour.

This is a full-time (35 hrs/week) hybrid position for 6 months with the option to extend. Our office is located in Downtown Brooklyn.
